2. Introduction

Economy - not pre-given by natural laws - is constructed as social set of co-living rules to allocate, distribute and share (natural and non-natural) resources. Ex-ante modelling economical systems by social rules (contractual, legislative, judicative, executive).

The state (situation, position) of the Economy can be described by e.g. Assets, Liabilities, Expenses, Incomes, Profitability, Productivity, etc.. The Economy changes through time from a state \(r_0=\) “start” to a state \(r=\) “end”. For the state to change, work is applied by processing limited resources (e.g. access to raw materials and availability of intermediate goods), constrained capital (e.g. financial funds, interest rates, and inflation), as well as labor capacity (e.g machines and human force).

How can a state \(r\) be modelled by work \(W\), which is applied for the change to occure: \(r = \int A = \int \int W\) ?



3. Work and Energy

\(\exists\) Work done or received \(W \neq 0 \Rrightarrow \exists\) Change of State (“Displacement”, “Deformation”) \(r \neq 0\):

\[\begin{split} & W = \partial A = \partial(E \cdot t) = \partial(p ⋅ r) \end{split} \tag{1}\]

Geometrical: \[\begin{split} & W = F \cdot r = mr \cdot a = I ⋅ \frac{v}{t} = \frac{p}{t} ⋅ tv = Ft \cdot v = pv = mv^2 \end{split} \tag{2}\]

Analytical: \[\begin{split} & W = \mathcal{L} + \mathcal{H} = (\mathcal{K} - \mathcal{U}) + (\mathcal{K} + \mathcal{U}) = (\mathcal{K} - (\mathcal{V} + \mathcal{Z})) + (\mathcal{K} + \mathcal{U}) = (\mathcal{K} - \mathcal{U}) + E \end{split} \tag{3}\]

Electro Magnetic: \[\begin{split} & W = \left( \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - q(\Phi - \vec{\mathcal{A}} \cdot \vec{v}) \right) + \left( \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{m} (\vec{p} - q\vec{\mathcal{A}})^2 + q\Phi \right)\end{split} \tag{4}\]

Relativistic: \[\begin{split} & W = \frac{v}{c} \sqrt{E^2 - (mc^2)^2} \end{split} \tag{5}\]

Thermal: \[\begin{split} & W = PV^{\iota} = T \cdot V^{\frac{f+2}{f}} = T \cdot V^{(\frac{c_P}{c_V}-1)} = T \cdot V^{\kappa-1} = T \cdot l \cdot g_0 = T \cdot n \cdot b_0 = T \cdot m \cdot c_0 \end{split} \tag{6}\]

Thermo Statistical: \[\begin{split} & W = ΔY - ΔH = \Delta(T \cdot \Delta S - P \cdot \Delta V) - \Delta(T\cdot S) \end{split} \tag{7}\]

Quantistic: \[\begin{split} & W = ħ\mathcal{k} \cdot ω = ℎν\mathcal{k} = E\mathcal{k} \end{split} \tag{8}\]

5.Hypothesis and Conjecture

Conjecture of Equation 17 and Equation 6

How consistently map economical measures to the quantities of nature to model the economical system?

Input:

\[\begin{split} & Input = f(Resouces, Labor, Capital) \text{ = Work = } \Delta Energy \end{split} \tag{19}\]

Output:

\[\begin{split} & Output = V_I \text{ = Goods + Services = Production = } \Delta State \end{split} \tag{20}\]

Productivity:

\[\begin{split} & Productivity = \frac{Output}{Input} = \frac{\Delta State}{Work} = \frac{r_1 - r_0}{F \cdot r_0} = \frac{V_I}{\Delta Energy} \end{split} \tag{21}\]



Economy \(\rightarrow\) \(\leftarrow\) Physics
Assets Resources Matter
Liabilities Capital Momentum Space
Expenses Labor Work
Income Production Energy Time



As a first approach, let’s take the geometrical perspective of the concept of “work” (Equation 2), applied to basic economical (monetary) measurements, so as to satisfy the following coupled system:

\(f_A(Assets) = f_L(Liabilities)\) = “momentum” \(p\) from initial state \(r_0\) to new state \(r_1\), and “state change” = \(r = r_1 - r_0\)

Assets = Property Capital (substance of matter, object movement/change) = \(m \cdot v\)
Liabilities = Financial Capital (space of movement, maneuverability) = \(m \cdot \frac{r}{t}\)
Expenses = \(m \cdot \frac{v}{t} \cdot r\) = \(\dot p \cdot r = F \cdot r\) = “work”
Income = \(\Delta E = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 + mv_0r\) = “energy change” = profit or loss

Thus, to be determined \(r\) = “state change through trajectory” (Balance substance change) by given \(v\) = “velocity of movement” (Business speed), or find \(v\) by given \(r\); where \(r\), \(v\), and \(v_0\) shall be modeled within the constraints of contractual agreement means and by the given juristic legislation.
