1 Abstract

An exploratory attempt …

Interconnection network between movement, force, momentum, energy, and work. Describing systems with Lagrangian and Hamltonian when constraints and starting conditions exist. Modeling systems and optimisation with constraints and conditions. Interwoven system of macro- and microeconomie under constraints, limitations, and conditions.



2 Introduction

Nature strives through Physics to behave economical by using its resources in an austere way: Principle of Least Action.

Economy - not pre-given by natural laws - is constructed as social set of co-living rules to allocate, distribute and share (natural and non-natural) resources. Ex-ante modelling economic systems by rules (contractual, legislative, judicative, executive).

The state (situation, position) of the Economy can be described by e.g. Assets, Liabilities, Expenses, Incomes, Profitability, Productivity, etc.. The Economy changes through time from a state \(r_0=\) “start” to a state \(r_1=\) “end”. For the state to change, work is applied by processing limited resources (e.g. access to raw materials and availability of intermediate goods), constrained capital (e.g. financial funds, interest rates, and inflation), as well as labor capacity (e.g machines and human force).

How can a trajectory \(r = r_1 - r_0\) between two states be modelled by work \(W\), which is applied for the change of the state to occure: \(r = \int A = \int \int W = \int \int (\mathcal{L} + \mathcal{H}) = \int \int (\Delta E - \Delta H)\) ?



3 Work and Energy

\(\exists\) Work done or received \(W \neq 0 \Rrightarrow \exists\) Change of State (“Displacement”, “Deformation”) \(r \neq 0\):

\[\begin{split} & W = \partial A = \partial(E \cdot t) = \partial(p ⋅ r) = F_1 \cdot r_1 + p_2 \cdot v_2 = \begin{cases} F \cdot r \\ p \cdot v \end{cases} \end{split} \tag{1}\]

Geometrical: \[\begin{split} & W = F \cdot r = mr \cdot a = I ⋅ \frac{v}{t} = \frac{p}{t} ⋅ tv = Ft \cdot v = pv = mv^2 \end{split} \tag{2}\]

Analytical: \[\begin{split} & W = p \cdot v = \mathcal{L} + \mathcal{H} = (\mathcal{K} - \mathcal{U}) + (\mathcal{K} + \mathcal{U}) = (\mathcal{K} - (\mathcal{V} + \mathcal{Z})) + (\mathcal{K} + \mathcal{U}) = (\mathcal{K} - \mathcal{U}) + E \end{split} \tag{3}\]

Electro Magnetic: \[\begin{split} & W = \mathcal{L} + \mathcal{H} = \left( \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - q(\Phi - \vec{\mathcal{A}} \cdot \vec{v}) \right) + \left( \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{m} (\vec{p} - q\vec{\mathcal{A}})^2 + q\Phi \right)\end{split} \tag{4}\]

Relativistic: \[\begin{split} & W = p \cdot v = \frac{v}{c} \sqrt{E^2 - (mc^2)^2} \end{split} \tag{5}\]

Thermal: \[\begin{split} & W = F \cdot r = PV^{\iota} = T \cdot V^{\frac{f+2}{f}} = T \cdot V^{(\frac{c_P}{c_V}-1)} = T \cdot V^{\kappa-1} = T \cdot l \cdot g_0 = T \cdot n \cdot b_0 = T \cdot m \cdot c_0 \end{split} \tag{6}\]

Thermo Statistical: \[\begin{split} & W = \mathcal{L} + \mathcal{H} = ΔU - ΔH = \Delta(T \cdot \Delta S - P \cdot \Delta V) - \Delta(T\cdot S) \end{split} \tag{7}\]

Quantistic:

\[\begin{split} & W = p \cdot v = ħ\mathcal{k} \cdot ω = ℎν\mathcal{k} = E\mathcal{k} \end{split} \tag{8}\]

\[\begin{split} & \Delta A = \Delta p \cdot \Delta r = \Delta E \cdot \Delta t \ge \frac{1}{2} \hbar \end{split} \tag{9}\]

4 Constrains and Conditions

Constraints given by:

  • initial state
  • boundry conditions
  • access to limited resources
  • contractual covenants
  • juristic law

Modelling movement (change of state) under constraints:

  1. Movement ist charecterized by change of energy, Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L} \neq 0\): \[\begin{split} & \mathcal{L} = E_{kin} - E_{pot} \\& \end{split} \tag{10}\]

  2. Movement happens within constraints of forces: \[\begin{split} & \hat{F_i} + \tilde{F_i} = \hat{F_i} + \sum_{k=1}^{s_d} \lambda_k a_{ki} = \frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial v_i} \right) - \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial r_i} \end{split} \tag{11}\]

    and at same time, constraining conditions delimitate movement: \[\begin{split} & \sum_{l=1}^f a_{kl} v_l + b_k = 0 \end{split} \tag{12}\]

  3. Rest is characterized by unchanged energy, Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H} = E_{kin} + E_{pot} = const.\). There is movement from state of rest if \(\mathcal{H} \neq 0\): \[\begin{split} & \mathcal{H} = \sum_i p_i v_i - \mathcal{L} = pv - \mathcal{L} = Fr - \mathcal{L} = W - \mathcal{L} = (\Delta E - \Delta H) - \mathcal{L} \end{split} \tag{13}\]

  4. Change of state/position is characterized by movement: \[\begin{split} & \dot r_i = v_i = \frac{\partial\mathcal{H}}{\partial p_i} \qquad \dot p_i = F_i = - \frac{\partial\mathcal{H}}{\partial r_i} \end{split} \tag{14}\]

see description of “work” Equation 2

where:

\(s_d =\) constraints   \(i = 1,...,f =\) dedrees of freedom   \(k = 1,...,s_d =\) constraints

\(\hat{F_i} =\) known constraining forces   \(\tilde{F_i} = \sum_{k=1}^{s_d} \lambda_k a_{ki} =\) unknown constraining forces

\(\lambda_k(t) =\) Eigenvalues for transformation of reference system

\(a_{ki} =\) Eigenvectors of transformation matrix

\(\lambda_k \cdot \vec r_i = \lambda_k \cdot \left[ a_{ki} \right]_{matrix}\)

6 Ex-ante Framing and Post-ante Optimization

Framing contracts and jurisdiction ahead to match \(p = \text{"Economy Funds"}\) and \(v = \text{"Economy Velocity"}\) that can be modelled with typical approaches for fuction \(z = \text{"arrival state"}\) depending on \(x = \text{"starting state"}\), so that “work” \(W = F \cdot r = F \cdot (z - x)\):



Linear optimization to achieve target states “\(z, h\)”:

  1. Oneness situaiton modelling:

    minimize efforts = \(z\) or maximizie return = \(z\)

\[\begin{split} & \text{target:} \hphantom{min.nstraints:} z = c^T \cdot x \\& \text{min. constraints:} \hphantom{max.} \left[ a_{ki} \right] \cdot x \ge b \\& \text{max. constraints:} \hphantom{min.} \left[ a_{ki} \right] \cdot x \le b \\& \text{condition:} \hphantom{max.raints:} x \ge 0 \\& \end{split} \tag{15}\]

  1. Duality situation modelling:

    minimize efforts = \(z\) and maximize return = \(h\)

\[\begin{split} & \text{target min.:} \hphantom{ andints:} z = c^T \cdot x \\& \text{min. constraints:} \hphantom{ and} \left[ a_{ki} \right] \cdot x \ge b \\& \text{condition:} \hphantom{ andraints:} x \ge 0 \\& \textit{ and} \\& \text{target max.:} \hphantom{ andints:} h = b^T \cdot y \\& \text{max. constraints:} \hphantom{ and} \left[ a_{ki} \right]^T \cdot y \le c \\& \text{condition:} \hphantom{ andraints:} y \ge 0 \\& \end{split} \tag{16}\]

  1. Normalization:

\[\begin{split} & \text{target:} \hphantom{min.nstraints:} z = c^T \cdot x \\& \text{min. constraints:} \hphantom{max.} \left( \left[ a_{ki} \right] - x_{n+k} \right) \cdot x = b \\& \text{max. constraints:} \hphantom{min.} \left( \left[ a_{ki} \right] + x_{n+k} \right) \cdot x = b \\& \text{condition:} \hphantom{max.raints:} x \ge 0 \\& \end{split} \tag{17}\]

7 Productiviy and Value

Society’s living requires:

  • “Resources” are processed to create goods to be consumed
  • “Labor” is used to process goods and services
  • “Capital” intermediates value between goods and services

Society organizes in groups of households:

  • natural households
  • juristic corporations
  • governmental institutions

Households cooperate though exchange (trade) of goods and services between “Demand” and “Supply” using monetary valuation:

  • Goods Market Production, Inventory, Trade \(\rightarrow\) “Price” for value of production
  • Labor Market Employment, Demographic Structure, Work Capabilities \(\rightarrow\) “Wage” for value of labor
  • Capital Market Savings, Investments, Exchange \(\rightarrow\) “Interest” for value of capital


acronyms see Section 9.2

Supply is provided by the income \(Y\):

\[\begin{split} & Y = L + Y_H + Y_A + T_A + Y_G - Z_G \end{split} \tag{18}\]

\[\begin{split} & Y = C + S + T + M - X \end{split} \tag{19}\]

Demand is satisfied by the production \(V_I\):

\[\begin{split} & V_I = C + I + G + X - M \end{split} \tag{20}\]

Equilibrium:

\[\begin{split} & Y = V_I \\& L - C = S = I \\& C + S = L \\& C + I = R \end{split} \tag{21}\]

Domestic Production & International Trade:

\[\begin{split} & V_S = Y + T - G_A + D_A \end{split} \tag{22}\]

\[\begin{split} & V_N = V_S - D_A \end{split} \tag{23}\]

\[\begin{split} & V_I = V_S - R_M + R_X \end{split} \tag{24}\]

Monetary Value of Production:

\[\begin{split} & P \cdot V_I = Q \cdot N \end{split} \tag{25}\]

Macro Productivity: \[\begin{split} & Productivity = \frac{Output}{Input} = \frac{V_I}{Y} \end{split} \tag{26}\]

We track production at microeconomic level through (acronyms see Section 9.2):

\(A =\) Assets represent “Property Capital” which consist of “Current Capital” and “Structural Capital”. Those investments are required for the production processes.

\(B =\) Liabilities represent “Financial Capital” which consist of “Exogen Funds” and “Endogen Funds”. Later includes “Equity Capital” and “Retained Gains”. Those funds do finance Assets.

\(W =\) Expenses represent “Work Efforts” to “Supply Production” with use of Assets.

\(Y =\) Income represents “Received Earnings” achieved with production to “Satisfy Demand”. Parts of the gains will be retained to finance Assets.

\(R =\) Gain, Profit or Loss from executed work (resultant value from input efforts) is represented by \(R = Y - W\)

The “transfer of energy” between all subsystems is mapped in balancing out theirs capital (usage of capital for production though possessed assets): \(A = B + R\)

Micro Productivity: \[\begin{split} & Productivity = \frac{Output}{Input} = \frac{Y}{W} \end{split} \tag{27}\]



8 Hypothesis and Conjecture

Conjecture of Equation 25 and Equation 6

How consistently map economic measures to the quantities of nature to model the economical system?

Input: \[\begin{split} & Input = f_0(Resouces, Labor, Capital) \text{ = Work = } \Delta\text{Energy} = p \cdot v \end{split} \tag{28}\]

Output: \[\begin{split} & Output \text{ = Goods + Services = Production = } \Delta\text{State} = V_I \end{split} \tag{29}\]

Productivity: \[\begin{split} & Productivity = \frac{Output}{Input} = \frac{\Delta State}{\Delta Energy} = \frac{Production}{Work} = \frac{V_I}{pv} \end{split} \tag{30}\]

see description of “movement” Equation 14





As a first approach, considering the geometrical perspective of the concept of “work” (Equation 2), applied to economical measurements, so to satisfy the following system:

\(f_A(Assets) = f_B(Liabilities + Gain)\) = “momentum” \(p\) from initial state \(r_0\) to new state \(r_1\), where the change of state is \(r = r_1 - r_0\)

Matter substance, object movement/change: \[\begin{split} & A = f_A = F \cdot t = p \end{split} \tag{31}\]

Space of movement, maneuverability: \[\begin{split} & B + R = f_B = m \cdot v = p \end{split} \tag{32}\]

Work input (efforts, costs) through labor force, machines and infrastructure applied, and materials consumed for production: \[\begin{split} & W = f_0 = F \cdot r = m \cdot \frac{v}{t} \cdot r = p \cdot v \end{split} \tag{33}\]

Energy transfer, energy change, profit or loss: \[\begin{split} & Y = \Delta E = \frac{1}{2} pv + \mathcal{U} \end{split} \tag{34}\]

Production value inflation adjusted: \[\begin{split} & P \cdot V_I = \sum Y_{adjust} = \sum (W + R)_{adjust} = \Delta U - \Delta H = Q \cdot N \end{split} \tag{35}\]

see Equation 7 and Equation 2

Thus, to be determined are \(v\) (economy velocity) and \(p\) (financial structure). Eventually, determine \(r\) = “state change through trajectory” (substance change to the new assets structure) by given \(v\) = “velocity of movement” (Business speed = monetary value, price with inflation), or determine \(v\) by given \(r\). The individual positions \(\{ i,j,l,m \}\) of Liabilities \(\{ B_i + R_j \}_k\), Assets \(\{ A_m \}_k\), and Expenses \(\{ W_l \}_k\) shall be grouped in sets \(\{ k \}\), so to satisfy \(f_k(i,j) = f_k(m) = f_k(l) = \{ pv \}_k\).

The constraints shall be modelled with the Lagrangian. Hence, “Work” shall be derived from the Hamiltonian which leads to the identification of the “movement” (= change, transformation) of the trajectory (= path to new state) with \(\dot r = v\) and \(\dot p = F\).

Experiment design: \(p\) and \(v\) are modelled ex-ante with means of CONSTRAINTS from contractual agreement and from given juristic legislation, in such a way that physics laws are satisfied. Decisions making occure within this ex-ante frame.
